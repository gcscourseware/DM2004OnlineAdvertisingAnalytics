Introduction to Online Advertising

What is Online Advertising?

Online advertising, also known as digital advertising or internet advertising, is the practice of delivering promotional marketing messages to consumers through internet and digital channels.

üéØ Key Characteristics

  • Digital Delivery: Advertisements are delivered through websites, apps, social media, and search engines
  • Targeted Approach: Ability to reach specific audiences based on demographics, interests, and behaviors
  • Measurable Results: Real-time tracking and analytics of campaign performance
  • Interactive: Users can engage directly with ads through clicks, views, and conversions

Types of Online Advertising

üîç Search Engine Marketing (SEM)

Paid advertisements that appear on search engine results pages (Google, Bing). Ads are triggered by specific keyword searches.

üì± Social Media Advertising

Sponsored content and ads on social platforms like Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter, and TikTok.

üñºÔ∏è Display Advertising

Visual banner ads that appear on websites, apps, and social media platforms. Includes images, animations, and rich media.

üé¨ Video Advertising

üìß Email Marketing

Advertising Platforms & Networks

What are Advertising Platforms?

Advertising platforms are digital services that connect advertisers with their target audiences. These platforms provide tools for creating, managing, and optimizing ad campaigns across various channels.

üîë Key Platform Features

  • Audience Targeting: Demographics, interests, behaviors, and custom audiences
  • Campaign Management: Budget control, scheduling, and bid strategies
  • Analytics & Reporting: Performance metrics, conversion tracking, and insights
  • Creative Tools: Ad builders, templates, and asset libraries

Google Ads

üîç The World's Largest Ad Platform

Google Ads reaches over 90% of global internet users through search, display, video, and app advertising.

Key Features & Benefits

Search Ads: Appear at the top of Google search results when users search for relevant keywords. Pay-per-click (PPC) model.
Display Network: Access to millions of websites, reaching 90% of internet users worldwide with visual ads.
YouTube Ads: Video advertising on the world's second-largest search engine with 2+ billion users.

Best For:

üéØ Businesses seeking high-intent customers, e-commerce stores, local businesses, B2B companies, and those with clear conversion goals.

Ad Formats and Their Uses

Text Ads

üìù Characteristics

Simple, text-based advertisements primarily used in search engine marketing. Consist of headline, description, and URL.

Best Uses: Search campaigns, high-intent targeting, direct response marketing, local businesses

Platforms: Google Search, Bing Ads, LinkedIn Text Ads

Key Benefit: Catches users actively searching for solutions, leading to higher conversion rates

Display Ads

üé® Characteristics

Visual banner advertisements featuring images, graphics, and text. Available in various sizes (leaderboard, rectangle, skyscraper).

Best Uses: Brand awareness, retargeting, visual storytelling, product showcases

Platforms: Google Display Network, Facebook/Instagram, programmatic ad networks

Key Benefit: Massive reach across millions of websites with strong visual impact

11/12

Video Ads

üé¨ The Most Engaging Format

Video ads generate 1200% more shares than text and images combined. They're the fastest-growing ad format globally.

Types of Video Ads

In-Stream Ads (Skippable): Pre-roll, mid-roll, or post-roll ads on YouTube and social platforms. Viewers can skip after 5 seconds. Pay only when viewers watch 30 seconds or engage.
Non-Skippable Ads: 15-20 second ads that must be watched before content. Higher completion rates but can frustrate viewers if not engaging.
Bumper Ads: 6-second non-skippable ads perfect for brand recall and mobile users. Great for reinforcing messages.
Stories & Reels: Vertical, full-screen video ads on Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok. Native format with high engagement rates.

Best Practices

‚úì Hook viewers in first 3 seconds | ‚úì Optimize for sound-off viewing | ‚úì Include clear call-to-action | ‚úì Keep it concise and engaging

Case Studies: Successful Ad Campaigns

üéØ Case Study 1: Airbnb - "Live There" Campaign

Platform: Facebook & Instagram Video Ads

Strategy: Shifted focus from tourist attractions to living like locals. Used user-generated content and authentic experiences in video storytelling.

Results: 14 million video views in first week, 6x increase in brand awareness, and significant boost in bookings across new markets.

Key Takeaway: Authentic storytelling with video content resonates deeply with audiences and drives both awareness and conversions.

üíº Case Study 2: Slack - LinkedIn B2B Campaign

Platform: LinkedIn Sponsored Content & InMail

Strategy: Targeted specific job titles in tech companies with case studies and ROI-focused messaging. Used lead gen forms for frictionless sign-ups.

Results: 35% increase in qualified leads, 50% lower cost per acquisition compared to other channels, 24% conversion rate from ad to trial.

Key Takeaway: LinkedIn's professional targeting delivers high-quality B2B leads when messaging focuses on business value.

üõçÔ∏è Case Study 3: Dollar Shave Club - Viral Video Marketing

Platform: YouTube & Display Network

Strategy: Created a humorous, low-budget launch video explaining their value proposition. Combined with targeted display retargeting.

Results: 27 million views, 12,000 orders in first 48 hours, company acquired for $1 billion by Unilever.

Key Takeaway: Creative, memorable content can achieve massive reach even with limited budgets, especially when it entertains while solving problems.
